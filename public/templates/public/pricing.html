What is the Blockchain Timestamp?
When emondo digitally signs a completed document a hash code is produced. This hash code is then published to the Ethereum (Blockchain) network where it is acknowledged by 1000’s of computers, timestamped and becomes part of a block in the Blockchain. This provides independent proof of a document's creation at a certain point of time.

What is the difference between an electronic signature and a digital signature?
The term electronic signature is a general meaning including all types of legally recognisable (not necessarily tangibly recognisable) signatures. Therefore, electronic signatures include digital signatures. In practice electronic signatures are manifested symbol(s) (e.g. your name typed, drawn or uploaded). The distinct characteristic of a digital signature is they require the use of asymmetric cryptography such as RSA or Elliptic Curve and Public Key Infrastructure (PKI) technology. Through these technologies a signatory digitally signs their name using a private key which provides additional security via the secrecy of the signatories unique private key.
What types of documents are electronic signatures not legally valid?
As a common rule of thumb wills, trust deeds and enduring POA’s (ex. LPOA’s) may not allow the use of electronic signatures.
